PFÄLZER Bodenbau & Sanierung GmbH Invoicing Software

A Detailed Step-by-Step Guide

A QUICK NOTE BEFORE YOU START: This is a multilingual program. This guide is written for the English interface.

To get the best experience, please select EN from the Language menu in the sidebar first. The screenshots in this guide are being updated, but the text instructions perfectly match what you'll see in the English version of the app.

Chapter 1: Welcome! Let's Look at Your Dashboard

Welcome aboard! This guide is here to walk you through using the software efficiently. Our goal is to make sure you understand what every feature does, without any struggle. The first screen that greets you is the Dashboard. Think of it as your business's main control panel, giving you a quick summary of everything.

What Do These Numbers on the Dashboard Mean?

  • Total Number of Customers: Shows how many clients you've saved in the system.
  • Total Number of Products: Tells you how many different services or products you've set up for invoicing.
  • Total Number of Invoices: The grand total of every invoice you've ever created.
  • Total Turnover: The total amount of money from all your invoices, including VAT.
  • Newest Customers List: A quick look at the latest clients you've added.
  • Newest Invoices List: Shows the last few invoices you created, so you can quickly check if they were saved correctly.

Chapter 2: The Most Important First Step: Your Company Settings

Before you can write your first invoice, there is one crucial, one-time setup you must complete: entering your own company's details. The information you put here will be automatically printed on every invoice you create, and it's legally required for them to be valid.

How to Fill In Your Company Details:

  1. From the main menu on the left, click on Settings.
  2. You will see a form like the one in the picture above.
  3. Carefully fill out all the fields (Company Name, Address, Phone, IBAN, Tax Number, etc.).
  4. Don't forget to click the Save Changes button at the bottom.
BE CAREFUL: If the information in this section is incorrect or incomplete, all the invoices you create might be legally invalid. Please double-check your details.

Chapter 3: Managing Your Customers

To send an invoice, you first need someone to send it to. In this section, you'll learn how to add, view, and manage your clients.

How to Add a New Customer:

  1. Open the Customers menu from the sidebar and click the New Customer link.
  2. The "New Customer Information" form will appear. Let's fill it out.
A Very Important Option: The "Reverse Charge" Checkbox

At the bottom of the form, you'll see a checkbox that says "Is the recipient liable for VAT in Germany?". This is one of the most important settings in the entire program.

Here's what it means in simple terms: Normally, you add 19% VAT to every invoice. However, a special tax law in Germany (called the "Reverse Charge" mechanism) sometimes means your client is responsible for paying the VAT, not you. This often applies in the construction industry.
  • You should check this box only if your customer has told you this rule applies to them. If you do, the program will automatically remove the 19% VAT from their invoices.
  • If you are not sure, leave this box empty. It's always best to ask your accountant if you don't know.

Step 3: After filling everything out, click the Add Customer button.

Your Customer List

By navigating to Customers > Customer List, you can see all your registered clients. You can delete a customer using the trash can icon on the right side of each entry.

Chapter 4: Managing Your Products & Services

If you do the same jobs repeatedly, you can save yourself a lot of time by adding them here first. Instead of typing the details every time, you can just select them from a list when you create an invoice.

How to Add a New Product or Service:

  1. Open the Products menu and click the New Product link.
A Very Important Option: The "Lump Sum" Checkbox

On this form, you'll find the "Is this a lump sum work?" checkbox.

Here's what it means in simple terms: A "lump sum" job is any work where you agree on a single, total package price, instead of charging by the hour or by the square meter. For example, if you agree to a price of €5,000 for a "complete bathroom renovation," that's a lump sum job.
  • If the work you're adding is a fixed-price package deal, check this box. When you add this service to an invoice, the program will automatically lock the quantity to '1'.
  • If you charge based on quantity (like per hour or per m²), leave this box empty.

Your Product List

Go to Products > Product List to see all your saved products and services. You can delete them with the trash can icon.

Chapter 5: Let's Create an Invoice!

This is what it's all about: creating accurate invoices, fast.

The Invoice List: All Your Invoices

Navigate to Invoices > Invoice List to see all your invoices and their payment status. On the right side of each invoice, you'll find 4 useful action icons.

What the Action Icons Mean:
  • View Details: Opens a summary page of the invoice. You can't edit here, just view.
  • Edit Invoice: Click this if you made a mistake or need to change something. It opens the invoice in the editor.
  • Delete Invoice: Permanently deletes the invoice. Don't worry, it will ask you "Are you sure?" first.
  • View PDF: Opens a new browser tab with the final, professional PDF version of your invoice, ready to be sent to your client.

Creating a New Invoice, Step by Step:

  1. From the menu, click on Invoices > Create New Invoice.

Step 2 (The Top Section): Fill in the general invoice details.

  • Select Customer: Choose your client from the dropdown list. This is important, as it will automatically decide if VAT should be applied!
  • Fill out the other fields like Invoice Number, Invoice Date, Due Date, Construction Site, etc.

Step 3 (The Middle Section): Add the jobs or items to the invoice.

  • Select Product/Service: Choose a service you saved earlier. The Unit and Unit Price fields will fill in for you automatically.
  • Quantity: Enter the amount of work you did (e.g., `50` for 50 m²).
  • Add Item: Click this button to add the job to the invoice list below. You can add as many items as you need.

Step 4 (The Bottom Section): Check the totals. The program does all the math for you, calculating the Subtotal, VAT, and Grand Total. If everything looks right, click the Create Invoice button at the very bottom.

Chapter 6: The Final Document and a Pro-Tip

You can use the entire program in your preferred language. The choice you make in the Language menu will change both the app interface and the language of the final PDF invoice.

Pro-Tip for Professional Invoices: If you need to add a line break in a long description on your PDF, simply type the `|` (vertical bar) character where you want the new line to start. The program will handle the rest, making your descriptions neat and easy to read.

After following all these steps, you will have a professional, legally-compliant PDF invoice that you can proudly send to your client.
